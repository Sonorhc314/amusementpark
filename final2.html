Discovery

Located in the Permian Basin desert of West Texas, there was once discovered a place as known as Mystery flesh pit by a common mining operation. By today it was turned into an amusent park. Once owned by the corporation Anodyne, today owned by a little French organisation called Esprit

Unique ecosystem

It is important to know that Mystery Flesh pit is a unique ecosystem. There is a vast number of species to be found nowhere else but there. Many creatures were once foreigners, that's a fact. Take for example these weasle-looking creature from the brochure, which after many years of having generations thriving in the Mystery flesh pit morphed into an ungainly clump of organs.

Years after the accident

As the documents tell: "At 9:41 PM CST on July 4th, 2007, the Permian Basin Superorganism Natural Preserve (known colloquially as the “Mystery Flesh Pit National Park”) experienced a catastrophic disaster which resulted in over 750 fatalities, and over 1,800 major injuries"

But don't worry! Esprit took measures to make the park safer than it ever was under Anodyne.
